## The clock

I have about a year before mandatory military service interrupts everything. The realistic question is not "can I become a senior engineer in a year" — it's "what can I ship in a year that a hiring manager can read in 30 seconds and believe."

The answer I settled on is: finish Phase 1 and Phase 2 of a self-built systems roadmap, with a capstone at the end of each.

## The phases

- **Phase 1 — C + Linux.** K&R cover-to-cover with every exercise. Then King's *C Programming* to fill in the gaps. Capstone: a toy shell with pipes, job control, signal handling.
- **Phase 2 — OS internals.** OSTEP (with the projects) and TLPI as a reference. Capstone: a mini container runtime in C — namespaces, cgroups v2, `pivot_root`, image extraction from a tarball.

Two capstones. Both readable in a README in under five minutes. Both impossible to fake.

## Why no certs

Cost. I'm in Iran; the CKA exam alone is more than the textbook stack I'd otherwise want to own. The substitute is to make the projects loud enough that a recruiter doesn't need the cert to take a chance.

## Cadence

Five days of core track per week, two days of game dev. Cold Promise stays alive as the creative recovery slot. Never let it go dark for more than two weeks. One blog post per phase, minimum.

This site is part of that loudness.
